## 1. Analogy Generator

**Use Case**: Explaining a complex topic using a simple analogy.

```
Explain the complex topic of [Complex Topic, e.g., "Blockchain technology", "Machine Learning", "Quantum Physics"] using a simple analogy related to [Simple Concept, e.g., "a public ledger", "a student learning", "a light switch"].

The explanation should be clear, concise, and easy for a complete beginner to understand.
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Topic:** Blockchain Technology
**Analogy:** A Google Doc

"Think of a blockchain like a shared Google Doc.
1.  **Shared & Distributed:** Everyone in the group has access to the same document. When someone makes a change, it updates for everyone.
2.  **Transparent:** Everyone can see all the changes and who made them.
3.  **Secure:** Instead of just being able to delete or change previous entries, new entries (blocks) are added to the end of the document in a way that is cryptographically linked to the previous one, making it incredibly difficult to tamper with older information without everyone noticing."
```

## 2. Recipe Creator

**Use Case**: Generating a recipe based on available ingredients.

```
Create a recipe based on the following:

Main Ingredients: [List 2-4 main ingredients you have, e.g., "Chicken breast, broccoli, rice"]
Cuisine Style: [e.g., "Italian", "Mexican", "Asian-inspired", "Quick and Easy"]
Dietary Restrictions: [e.g., "Gluten-free", "Vegetarian", "Low-carb"]
Available Kitchen Tools: [e.g., "Oven, stovetop, air fryer, blender"]

The recipe should include:
- A creative name for the dish.
- A list of all ingredients (including common pantry staples like oil, salt, pepper).
- Step-by-step instructions.
- Estimated prep and cook time.
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Name:** Quick Lemon Herb Chicken & Broccoli

**Prep time:** 10 mins
**Cook time:** 20 mins

**Ingredients:**
- 2 chicken breasts
- 1 head of broccoli
- 1 cup of rice
- 1 lemon
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- Salt and pepper

**Instructions:**
1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. Cut broccoli into florets and chop chicken into bite-sized pieces.
3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook until browned.
4. Add broccoli to the skillet. Season with oregano, salt, and pepper. Squeeze the juice of half a lemon over the top.
5. Cook for 5-7 minutes, until broccoli is tender-crisp.
6. Serve chicken and broccoli over rice.
```

## 3. Fictional Story Starter

**Use Case**: Overcoming writer's block by generating a compelling story starter.

```
Create an engaging opening paragraph for a fictional story.

Genre: [e.g., "Science Fiction", "Fantasy", "Mystery", "Thriller", "Romance"]
Key Elements to Include:
- A character: [e.g., "An old detective", "A young sorceress"]
- A setting: [e.g., "A neon-lit cyberpunk city", "An ancient, enchanted forest"]
- A mysterious object or event: [e.g., "A watch that runs backward", "A sudden, unnatural silence"]

The paragraph should be no more than 150 words and end on a hook that makes the reader want to know what happens next.
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Genre:** Mystery
**Character:** An old detective
**Setting:** A neon-lit cyberpunk city
**Object:** A watch that runs backward

The rain never stopped in Neo-Kyoto, a city that had forgotten the sun. For retired detective Kaito, every drop that slid down his apartment window was a memory he'd rather forget. He was polishing his old service revolver, a relic in an age of plasma casters, when the package arrived. It was small, wrapped in plain brown paper, with no return address. Inside was a single item: an elegant, silver pocket watch. Kaito's blood ran cold as he watched the second hand tick silently, unnaturally, backward.
```

## 5. Workout Plan Creator

**Use Case**: Designing a custom workout plan.

```
Create a workout plan for a week.

Fitness Goal: [e.g., "Build muscle", "Lose weight", "Improve cardiovascular health"]

Time per Workout: [e.g., 30 minutes, 60 minutes]
Available Equipment: [e.g., "Dumbbells", "Treadmill", "Bodyweight only"]
Days per Week: [e.g., 3 days, 5 days]

The plan should specify:
- The workout for each day (e.g., "Day 1: Upper Body", "Day 2: Lower Body", "Day 3: Cardio").

**Day 1: Upper Body**

- Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10 reps
- Overhead Press: 3 sets of 8 reps
- Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 12 reps

**Day 2: Lower Body**
- Goblet Squats: 3 sets of 12 reps
- Lunges: 3 sets of 10 reps per leg
- Glute Bridges: 3 sets of 15 reps
- Calf Raises: 3 sets of 20 reps

**Day 3: Full Body & Core**
- Dumbbell Thrusters: 3 sets of 10 reps
- Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds
- Russian Twists: 3 sets of 15 reps per side
```

## 8. Personalized Travel Itinerary

**Use Case**: Creating a custom travel itinerary.

```
Create a travel itinerary for a [Number of Days]-day trip to [City/Country].

Traveler Profile:
- Interests: [e.g., "History and museums", "Food and nightlife", "Outdoor adventure", "Relaxing on the beach"]
- Budget: [e.g., "Budget-conscious backpacker", "Mid-range", "Luxury"]
- Pace: [e.g., "Fast-paced and packed", "Relaxed with lots of downtime"]

The itinerary should be a day-by-day plan that includes:
- Recommendations for activities and sights for the morning, afternoon, and evening.
- Suggestions for meals (e.g., "Try the street food at [Market Name]," "Book a reservation at [Restaurant Name]").
- Transportation tips (e.g., "Use the subway system," "Rent a scooter").
```

**Example Output**:
```
**3-Day Trip to Rome for a History Buff on a Mid-Range Budget**

**Day 1: Ancient Rome**
- **Morning:** Explore the Colosseum (book tickets online in advance to skip the line).
- **Afternoon:** Walk through the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill.
- **Evening:** Dinner in the Trastevere neighborhood. Try a classic Carbonara.

**Day 2: Vatican City**
- **Morning:** Visit St. Peter's Basilica and climb the dome for an incredible view.
- **Afternoon:** Explore the Vatican Museums and the Sistine Chapel (again, book tickets weeks in advance).
- **Evening:** A relaxed dinner near the Vatican.

**Day 3: Renaissance & Baroque Rome**
- **Morning:** Visit the Pantheon and toss a coin in the Trevi Fountain.
- **Afternoon:** Explore the art at the Borghese Gallery and Museum (reservations are mandatory).
- **Evening:** Enjoy the atmosphere at the Spanish Steps and Piazza Navona.
```

## 9. Role-playing Scenario Generator

**Use Case**: Practicing for a difficult conversation or interview by role-playing.

```
Create a role-playing scenario to practice for a [Situation].

Situation: [e.g., "Asking for a raise", "Negotiating a contract with a client", "Giving constructive feedback to a direct report"]

Your Role: [Your position in the scenario]
The Other Person's Role & Personality: [e.g., "A busy, data-driven boss", "A skeptical client focused on price", "A sensitive employee who doesn't take feedback well"]

The prompt should generate:
- A brief setup of the scenario.
- A list of potential opening lines for the other person.
- A list of potential objections or difficult questions they might raise during the conversation.
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Situation:** Asking for a raise.
**Your Role:** A top-performing employee who has been at the company for 3 years.
**Other Person:** A busy, data-driven boss.

**Scenario Setup:**
You have scheduled a 30-minute meeting with your boss to discuss your career growth and compensation. You have prepared a document outlining your key achievements from the past year.

**Potential Opening Lines from Your Boss:**
- "Hi [Your Name], thanks for meeting. What's on your mind?"
- "Things are hectic today, so let's get right to it. What's this about?"

**Potential Objections/Questions:**
- "As you know, budgets are tight this quarter."
- "Your performance has been great, but we need to keep compensation consistent across the team."
- "How does your request compare to the market rate for your role?"
- "Let me think about it. I'll need to discuss this with HR."
```

## 10. Creative Writing Prompter

**Use Case**: Generating a unique and challenging prompt for creative writing exercises.

```
Generate a creative writing prompt that combines three unrelated elements.

Element 1 (A Character): [e.g., "A retired librarian"]
Element 2 (A Setting): [e.g., "A space station orbiting a black hole"]
Element 3 (An Object): [e.g., "A cursed locket"]

Combine these elements into a single, intriguing paragraph that sets up a story.
```

**Example Output**:
```
Elara, the last librarian of the Alexandria Interstellar Archives, had spent her life cataloging dying stars and forgotten nebulae. Her quiet existence aboard the 'Stellifer' station, perpetually balanced on the edge of the Cygnus X-1 black hole, was one of predictable solitude. That changed the day a derelict freighter, silent for a thousand years, drifted out of the accretion disk. Its only cargo was a small, intricately carved locket. The moment Elara touched it, the hum of the station's life support ceased, and a faint, feminine whisper echoed in the sudden, terrifying silence.
```

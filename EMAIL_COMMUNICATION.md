## 1. Professional Email Response Generator

**Use Case**: Responding to customer inquiries, vendor questions, or internal communications

```
Write a professional email response to: [CUSTOMER MESSAGE]

Context:
- Company: [YOUR COMPANY]
- Your role: [YOUR TITLE] 
- Tone: [Professional/Friendly/Formal]
- Key points to address: [MAIN POINTS]
- Next steps: [WHAT YOU WANT TO HAPPEN]

Make the response helpful, clear, and actionable. Include appropriate greeting and closing.
```

**Example Output**:

```
Subject: Re: Question about your software pricing

Hi Sarah,

Thank you for your interest in our project management software.

To answer your question about pricing: Our standard plan is $29/month per user, which includes all core features plus 24/7 support. For teams over 10 users, we offer volume discounts starting at 15% off.

I'd be happy to schedule a 15-minute demo to show how it works for teams like yours. Are you available this week for a quick call?

Best regards,
Mike Johnson
Sales Manager, TechCorp
```

## 12. Difficult Feedback Delivery Email

**Use Case**: Structuring an email to deliver constructive but difficult feedback to a colleague or direct report, often as a prelude to a conversation.

```
Draft an email to schedule a meeting to deliver difficult feedback.

Context:
- Employee/Colleague Name: [Name]
- Topic of Feedback: [High-level topic, e.g., "communication style in team meetings", "missed deadlines on Project X"]
- Goal of Conversation: [e.g., "To discuss observations and create a plan for improvement"]
- Desired Tone: [Supportive, constructive, and forward-looking]

The email should not contain the specific feedback itself, but rather set the stage for a private, constructive conversation.
```

**Example Output**:

```
Subject: Quick chat to discuss Project X

Hi [Name],

I'd like to schedule a 30-minute one-on-one meeting with you tomorrow to discuss your work on Project X. I want to go over a few of my observations and, most importantly, talk about how I can best support you in your role.

Please let me know what time on your calendar works for a private video call.

Best,
[Your Manager's Name]
```

## 14. Apology Email to a Customer or Client

**Use Case**: Acknowledging a company mistake and outlining the steps to make it right.

```
Write a sincere and professional apology email to a client.

Context:
- Client Name: [Client Name]
- The Mistake: [Clearly and concisely describe what went wrong, e.g., "We missed the deadline for the V2 launch"]
- The Impact: [Acknowledge the negative impact on the client]
- Taking Responsibility: [Explicitly take responsibility without making excuses]
- The Fix: [What specific steps are you taking to correct the issue?]
- The Prevention: [What will you do to ensure this doesn't happen again?]
```

**Example Output**:

```
Subject: An Apology and Update Regarding Your Account

Hi [Client Name],

I am writing to personally apologize for the error on our part that led to the V2 launch delay. We pride ourselves on hitting our deadlines, and we failed to do so in this case. I know this has impacted your marketing plans, and for that, I am truly sorry.

The issue was caused by an unforeseen technical bug that has now been resolved. To make things right, we are providing a 25% credit on your next month's invoice.

We have also implemented a new pre-launch testing protocol to ensure this does not happen again.

We deeply value your partnership.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## 15. Delegating a Task via Email

**Use Case**: Clearly and effectively delegating a task to a team member.

```
Draft an email to delegate a task.

Context:
- Team Member's Name: [Name]
- The Task: [Name of the task]
- Background & Context: [Why is this task important?]
- Key Instructions & Requirements: [Provide a step-by-step guide or clear specifications]
- Deadline: [When does this need to be completed?]
- Required Resources: [Links to documents, tools, or other people they might need]
- Your Role: [Explain how you will support them, e.g., "I am here to answer any questions"]
```

**Example Output**:

```
Subject: Delegating: Q3 Competitor Analysis Report

Hi [Name],

I'd like to delegate the creation of the Q3 Competitor Analysis Report to you. This report is critical for our upcoming strategy session.

**Task:**
Please analyze our top 3 competitors and create a report summarizing their key activities in Q3.

**Instructions:**
- Use the template located here: [Link to template]
- Focus on their new feature launches, marketing campaigns, and pricing changes.
- The final report should be a 5-slide presentation.

**Deadline:** End of Day, [Date]

I am here to answer any questions you may have. Please let me know if you foresee any issues.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

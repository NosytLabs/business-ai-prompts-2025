## 1. Market Entry Strategy Analyzer

**Use Case**: Evaluating new market opportunities

```
Analyze market entry strategy for:

New Market: [TARGET MARKET]
Your Business: [CURRENT BUSINESS]
Proposed Product/Service: [WHAT YOU'LL OFFER]
Investment Budget: [AVAILABLE CAPITAL]
Timeline: [WHEN YOU WANT TO ENTER]
Risk Tolerance: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]

Provide analysis on:

MARKET ASSESSMENT:
- Market size and growth potential
- Customer segments and needs
- Competitive landscape
- Regulatory environment
- Cultural considerations

ENTRY STRATEGIES:
- Direct investment vs partnerships
- Organic growth vs acquisition
- Market penetration approach
- Distribution channels
- Pricing strategy

RISK ANALYSIS:
- Market risks
- Operational risks
- Financial risks
- Competitive responses
- Mitigation strategies

RECOMMENDATION:
- Go/No-Go decision
- Entry timeline
- Resource requirements
- Success metrics
- Exit strategy
```

**Example Output**:
```
**RECOMMENDATION for entering the German E-Bike Market:**

- **Go/No-Go:** Go. The market is large and growing, and our product's focus on long-range batteries is a key differentiator.
- **Entry Strategy:** Phased approach. Start with a direct-to-consumer e-commerce model to build brand recognition, then partner with local bike shops for distribution in Year 2. Avoid costly direct investment in physical stores initially.
- **Risk Mitigation:** Hire a local marketing manager to navigate cultural nuances. Offer a 30-day money-back guarantee to reduce customer hesitation.
```

## 2. Business Model Innovation Generator

**Use Case**: Reimagining how your business creates and captures value

```
Innovate the business model for:

Current Business: [EXISTING MODEL]
Industry: [YOUR SECTOR]
Core Challenges: [WHAT'S NOT WORKING]
Customer Feedback: [PAIN POINTS]
Competitive Pressure: [MARKET FORCES]
Technology Opportunities: [AVAILABLE TECH]

Explore these model innovations:

REVENUE MODEL ALTERNATIVES:
- Subscription vs one-time payment
- Freemium vs premium
- Usage-based vs fixed pricing
- Platform vs direct sales
- Service vs product focus

VALUE PROPOSITION EVOLUTION:
- Outcome-based vs feature-based
- Community vs individual value
- Ecosystem vs standalone solution
- Experience vs product focus
- Prevention vs cure approach

OPERATING MODEL OPTIONS:
- Asset-light vs asset-heavy
- Direct vs marketplace model
- B2B vs B2C vs B2B2C
- Local vs global focus
- Vertical vs horizontal integration

Provide 3 innovative model options with pros/cons and implementation complexity.
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Innovative Models for a Traditional Accounting Firm:**

**1. Subscription Model:**
- **Description:** Instead of hourly billing, clients pay a flat monthly fee for a package of services (e.g., bookkeeping, tax prep, advisory).
- **Pros:** Predictable revenue, stronger client relationships.
- **Cons:** Risk of scope creep, requires careful service packaging.
- **Complexity:** Medium.

**2. "CFO-as-a-Service" Model:**
- **Description:** Target startups and SMBs with a premium service that provides a dedicated part-time CFO for strategic guidance.
- **Pros:** High-margin service, positions the firm as a strategic partner.
- **Cons:** Requires a different skillset, smaller target market.
- **Complexity:** High.

**3. Platform Model:**
- **Description:** Build a platform that connects clients with a network of freelance accountants, with the firm taking a percentage and providing quality control.
- **Pros:** Highly scalable, asset-light.
- **Cons:** High technology investment, potential for channel conflict.
- **Complexity:** High.
```

## 5. Crisis Recovery Strategist

**Use Case**: Rebuilding business after major disruption

```
Develop crisis recovery strategy for:

Crisis Type: [WHAT HAPPENED]
Business Impact: [DAMAGE ASSESSMENT]
Current Resources: [WHAT YOU HAVE LEFT]
Stakeholders Affected: [WHO'S IMPACTED]
Recovery Timeline: [HOW LONG TO RECOVER]
Market Conditions: [EXTERNAL ENVIRONMENT]

IMMEDIATE STABILIZATION:
- Cash flow management
- Critical operations maintenance
- Stakeholder communication
- Emergency cost reductions
- Key relationship preservation

RECOVERY PHASES:

Phase 1: Stabilize (0-3 months)
- Stop the bleeding
- Secure essential operations
- Communicate with stakeholders
- Assess full damage
- Plan recovery approach

Phase 2: Rebuild (3-12 months)
- Restore core capabilities
- Rebuild customer confidence
- Strengthen financial position
- Optimize operations
- Prepare for growth

Phase 3: Transform (12+ months)
- Emerge stronger
- Implement lessons learned
- Build resilience
- Pursue new opportunities
- Establish competitive advantages

SUCCESS FACTORS:
- Leadership visibility
- Transparent communication
- Stakeholder engagement
- Financial discipline
- Operational excellence
- Cultural resilience

MONITORING METRICS:
- Financial recovery indicators
- Operational performance
- Customer satisfaction
- Employee engagement
- Market position
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Crisis Recovery Strategy for a Restaurant after a Fire**

**Crisis Type:** Physical asset destruction (kitchen fire).

**IMMEDIATE STABILIZATION (First 72 hours):**
- **Cash Flow:** Immediately contact the insurance company to start the claim process.
- **Communication:** Post a notice on the website and social media informing customers of a temporary closure.
- **Operations:** Secure the site to prevent further damage.

**RECOVERY PHASES:**
- **Phase 1: Stabilize (0-3 months):**
    - Secure an emergency loan to cover payroll.
    - Operate a limited menu out of a rented food truck to maintain brand presence and some revenue.
- **Phase 2: Rebuild (3-12 months):**
    - Oversee the reconstruction of the kitchen.
    - Launch a marketing campaign for the "Grand Re-opening."
- **Phase 3: Transform (12+ months):**
    - Use the rebuild as an opportunity to upgrade to more efficient kitchen equipment.
    - Implement lessons learned in a new, more robust fire safety plan.
```

## 15. Exit Strategy Planning

```

Company Information:
- Current Annual Revenue: [$$]
- Growth Rate: [% per year]
```

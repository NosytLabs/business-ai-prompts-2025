## 1. Sales Proposal Generator

**Use Case**: Creating professional proposals for prospects

```
Create a sales proposal for:

Prospect: [COMPANY/PERSON NAME]
Their challenge: [PROBLEM THEY NEED SOLVED]
Your solution: [HOW YOU CAN HELP]
Scope of work: [WHAT YOU'LL DELIVER]
Timeline: [HOW LONG IT TAKES]
Investment: [PRICING STRUCTURE]
Your credentials: [WHY THEY SHOULD CHOOSE YOU]

Structure:
1. Executive Summary
2. Problem Understanding
3. Proposed Solution
4. Timeline & Deliverables
5. Investment & Terms
6. Next Steps

Keep it client-focused and professional.
```

**Example Output**:
```
PROPOSAL: Website Redesign for ABC Manufacturing

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
Based on our conversation, ABC Manufacturing needs a modern website that better represents your 25-year reputation and generates more qualified leads. Our proposal outlines a complete redesign that will improve user experience and increase conversions.

PROBLEM UNDERSTANDING:
- Current website looks outdated (last updated 2018)
- Mobile experience is poor (40% of your traffic is mobile)
- Lead generation is minimal (currently 2-3 leads/month)
- Doesn't showcase your manufacturing capabilities effectively

...
```

## 2. Customer Objection Handler

**Use Case**: Responding to common sales objections

```
Help me respond to this customer objection:

Objection: [WHAT THE CUSTOMER SAID]
Product/service: [WHAT YOU'RE SELLING]
Customer context: [THEIR SITUATION]
Your value proposition: [WHY YOU'RE WORTH IT]
Competitive landscape: [OTHER OPTIONS THEY HAVE]

Provide:
1. Acknowledgment of their concern
2. Clarifying questions to understand better
3. Evidence-based response
4. Next step suggestion

Tone should be understanding, not pushy.
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Objection:** "Your price is 20% higher than your competitor, [Competitor Name]."

**Response Script:**
1.  **Acknowledge:** "I appreciate you sharing that, and it's a very fair point. Price is definitely an important factor."
2.  **Clarify:** "To make sure I understand, besides the price difference, does our solution seem like it would meet your needs effectively?"
3.  **Respond with Evidence:** "That 20% difference is where our value really comes in. It accounts for our dedicated 24/7 support, our 99.9% uptime guarantee, and our included onboarding program, which [Competitor Name] charges extra for. Our clients typically see a positive ROI within 6 months because of this higher level of service."
4.  **Next Step:** "Would it be helpful to walk through a detailed ROI calculation for your specific situation?"
```

## 3. Follow-Up Sequence Generator

**Use Case**: Staying in touch with prospects without being annoying

```
Create a follow-up sequence for:

Initial interaction: [HOW YOU MET THEM]
Their interest level: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW]
Decision timeline: [WHEN THEY'RE BUYING]
Key decision factors: [WHAT MATTERS TO THEM]
Competition: [WHO ELSE THEY'RE CONSIDERING]
Your differentiators: [WHY CHOOSE YOU]

Create 5 follow-up touchpoints over [TIME PERIOD] with:
- Timing for each touchpoint
- Value-add content for each
- Clear call-to-action
- Multiple communication channels
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Follow-up Sequence: 30 Days**

*   **Touchpoint 1 (Day 3 - Email):**
    *   **Value:** Send a relevant case study showing how a similar company achieved results.
    *   **CTA:** "Does this resonate with the challenges you're facing?"

*   **Touchpoint 2 (Day 7 - LinkedIn):**
    *   **Value:** Engage with their recent post or share an article relevant to their industry.
    *   **CTA:** (Soft) No direct ask, just building rapport.

*   **Touchpoint 3 (Day 14 - Email):**
    *   **Value:** Provide a short, helpful tip or a link to a free tool that addresses one of their pain points.
    *   **CTA:** "I had a thought about how we could solve [specific problem] for you. Are you free for a 15-min call next week?"

*   **Touchpoint 4 (Day 21 - Phone Call):**
    *   **Value:** A brief check-in call.
    *   **CTA:** "Hi [Name], just calling to follow up on my email. Is now still a good time?"

*   **Touchpoint 5 (Day 30 - Email):**
    *   **Value:** The "break-up" email.
    *   **CTA:** "It seems now might not be the right time. I won't follow up again, but please feel free to reach out if things change. Wishing you the best."
```

## 4. Customer Support Script

**Use Case**: Handling common customer service situations

```
Create a customer support script for:

Situation: [CUSTOMER ISSUE]
Customer emotion: [FRUSTRATED/CONFUSED/ANGRY]
Desired outcome: [WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE]
Company policy: [RELEVANT POLICIES]
Solution options: [WHAT YOU CAN OFFER]
Escalation path: [WHEN TO ESCALATE]

Include:
1. Empathetic opening
2. Information gathering questions
3. Solution presentation
4. Follow-up commitment
5. Satisfaction check
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Situation:** Customer received a damaged product.
**Customer Emotion:** Frustrated.

**Script:**
1.  **Opening:** "Hello, thank you for calling [Your Company]. My name is [Your Name]. I'm very sorry to hear that your order arrived damaged. I can definitely help you with that."
2.  **Info Gathering:** "To pull up your order, could I please get your name and order number?"
3.  **Solution:** "Okay, I see the order. I sincerely apologize for the inconvenience. I can ship you a replacement right away, which should arrive in 2-3 business days. There's no need for you to return the damaged item. How does that sound?"
4.  **Follow-up:** "I've processed the replacement order for you. You'll receive a confirmation email with the new tracking number within the hour."
5.  **Satisfaction Check:** "Is there anything else I can assist you with today? I want to make sure we've fully resolved this for you."
```

## 5. Pricing Conversation Guide

**Use Case**: Discussing price with prospects confidently

```
Help me discuss pricing for:

Product/service: [WHAT YOU'RE SELLING]
Your price: [WHAT YOU CHARGE]
Typical customer budget: [WHAT THEY EXPECT TO PAY]
Value delivered: [BENEFITS THEY GET]
ROI/payback period: [FINANCIAL RETURN]
Competitive pricing: [HOW YOU COMPARE]

Create a script that:
1. Builds value before mentioning price
2. Presents price confidently
3. Handles budget concerns
4. Offers payment options if appropriate
5. Reinforces value after price reveal
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Script:**
1.  **Build Value:** "Based on our discussion, it seems like our solution can save your team about 10 hours per week and reduce project errors by about 25%. Over a year, that's a significant impact on both productivity and your bottom line."
2.  **Present Price:** "With that in mind, the all-inclusive investment for the Pro plan, which includes everything we've discussed, is $499 per month."
3.  **Handle Concerns (Pause for reaction):** (If they say it's too high) "I understand. It's a significant investment. Can you tell me more about how this compares to the budget you had in mind?"
4.  **Offer Options:** "We also have a Standard plan at $299 per month that includes the core features, though it doesn't have the advanced automation we discussed."
5.  **Reinforce Value:** "I'm confident that the Pro plan will pay for itself within the first 4 months through the efficiency gains alone."
```

## 8. Lost Deal Analysis

**Use Case**: Learning from deals that didn't close

```
Analyze why this deal was lost:

Prospect: [WHO WE LOST]
Deal size: [POTENTIAL VALUE]
Sales process: [STEPS WE TOOK]
Competitor chosen: [WHO THEY PICKED]
Stated reason: [WHY THEY SAID NO]
Real reason (guess): [WHAT WE THINK HAPPENED]
Timing: [DECISION TIMELINE]

Provide:
1. Likely reasons for loss
2. What we could have done differently
3. Lessons for future deals
4. Whether to stay in touch
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Prospect:** Acme Corp
**Competitor Chosen:** CompetitorX

**1. Likely Reasons for Loss:**
- **Price:** CompetitorX was 15% cheaper.
- **Feature Gap:** They had a specific integration with [Software] that we lack.
- **Internal Champion:** Their main contact had a stronger relationship with the decision-maker.

**2. What We Could Have Done Differently:**
- Better quantified the ROI to offset the price difference.
- Introduced our CTO earlier to build a stronger technical relationship.
- Provided a clearer roadmap for the missing integration.

**3. Lessons for Future Deals:**
- For deals over $50k, always bring in an executive sponsor.
- Create a battle card for handling the CompetitorX objection.

**4. Stay in Touch?:** Yes. Set a reminder to check in in 6 months with a value-add article.
```

## 9. Customer Satisfaction Survey

**Use Case**: Getting feedback to improve service

```
Create a customer satisfaction survey for:

Service provided: [WHAT YOU DELIVERED]
Customer type: [B2B/B2C/INDUSTRY]
Relationship length: [HOW LONG THEY'VE BEEN A CUSTOMER]
Key touchpoints: [WHERE THEY INTERACT WITH YOU]
Improvement areas: [WHAT YOU WANT TO LEARN ABOUT]
Survey length: [SHORT/MEDIUM/DETAILED]

Include:
- Mix of rating scales and open-ended questions
- Questions about specific service aspects
- Net Promoter Score question
- Improvement suggestions
- Future needs assessment
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Subject: How did we do? Share your feedback in 2 minutes.**

Hi [Customer Name],

Thank you for being a valued customer. We're always working to improve our service, and your feedback is essential.

**1. On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Your Company] to a friend or colleague?** (Net Promoter Score)

**2. How satisfied were you with the [Service Provided]?** (Scale: Very Satisfied to Very Dissatisfied)

**3. What is one thing we could do to improve your experience?** (Open-ended)

**4. Are there any additional services you wish we offered?** (Open-ended)

Thank you for your time!

The [Your Company] Team
```

## 10. Win-Back Campaign

**Use Case**: Re-engaging lost or inactive customers

```
Create a win-back campaign for:

Lost customers: [WHY THEY LEFT]
Time since last purchase: [HOW LONG AGO]
Previous relationship: [WHAT THEY BOUGHT BEFORE]
Reason for leaving: [KNOWN ISSUES]
Changes since they left: [IMPROVEMENTS MADE]
Incentive budget: [WHAT YOU CAN OFFER]

Campaign should:
1. Acknowledge the relationship gap
2. Highlight improvements made
3. Offer compelling reason to return
4. Make it easy to re-engage
5. Show you value the relationship
```

**Example Output**:
```
**Campaign: 3-Email Sequence**

**Email 1 (Day 1): We've missed you!**
- **Subject:** It's been a while...
- **Body:** Acknowledge their absence. "We noticed you haven't been active in a while." Highlight 2-3 key improvements since they left. "Since you've been gone, we've added [Feature 1] and [Feature 2]."
- **CTA:** "See what's new." (Link to product).

**Email 2 (Day 5): Special Offer**
- **Subject:** A special offer to welcome you back
- **Body:** Offer a compelling, time-sensitive incentive. "As a thank you for your past business, here's 25% off your next purchase for the next 7 days."
- **CTA:** "Claim Your Discount."

**Email 3 (Day 10): Last Chance / Feedback Request**
- **Subject:** Is this goodbye?
- **Body:** A final, gentle nudge. "If we're no longer a good fit, would you be willing to share why in a quick 1-minute survey? Your feedback is invaluable."
- **CTA:** "Share Feedback" or "Return to My Account".
```

## 15. Handling an Angry Customer on Social Media

**Use Case**: De-escalating a public complaint on a platform like Twitter or Facebook.

```
Create a script for responding to an angry customer on social media.

Complaint Details:
- Platform: [Twitter/Facebook/etc.]
- The Public Complaint: [Summary of their post]
- Desired Action: [Move the conversation to a private channel]

The response should be:
1.  Fast and public.
2.  Empathetic and acknowledging.
3.  Does NOT resolve the issue in public.
4.  Provides a clear path to private resolution (e.g., "Please DM us your order number").
```
